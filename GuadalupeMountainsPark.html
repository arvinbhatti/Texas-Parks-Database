Guadalupe Mountains National Park

Description

Guadalupe Mountains National Park protects the world's most extensive Permian fossil reef, the four highest peaks in Texas, an environmentally diverse collection of flora and fauna, and the stories of lives shaped through conflict, cooperation and survival. Come experience mountains and canyons, desert and dunes, night skies and spectacular vistas within a place unlike any other.

Operating Hours
  • 8:00 AM - 4:30 PM Daily
Size
  • 86,367 acres

Campgrounds

  • Pine Springs Campground
  • Dog Canyon Campground

Reservations

There is no reservation system. Individual campsites are on a first come, first served basis.
